TechHire Miami

In March of 2015 the White House launched the TechHire Initiative, a Multi-Sector Effort and Call to Action to Give Americans Pathways to Well-Paying Technology Jobs.

In the fall of 2015 the TechHire Initiative in conjunction with the U.S. Department of Labor released a Grant for Applications supported by $100M in funding to Give Americans Pathways to Well-Paying Technology Jobs. A number of community leaders in Miami along with CareerSource South Florida and the Miami-Dade County Office of Management and Budget will be working together to apply for this ground breaking opportunity. The core focus of this grant application is to serve the needs of 17-29 year olds with barriers to training and employment. The target amount for the grant application will be under $4M.

Miami has already achieved success in giving its residents a pathway to well-paying technology jobs and now has an opportunity to utilize additional funding to create a long term, strong foundation that can serve many more people and companies. This foundation will be built on:

  • Inclusiveness
  • Measureable outcomes
  • Community and employer engagement
  • Focus on technology skills and careers

The ongoing successful leadership of CareerSource South Florida combined with innovative new training and employer engagement models has already established Miami as a leader in this arena. We now have the opportunity to broaden the focus and provide our increasingly diverse and growing community with greater opportunity for in demand technology careers.

TechHire Miami

Goals

TechHire Miami will be successful by including the core components of Inclusiveness, Measurable outcomes, Community and employer engagement, and A strong focus on in demand technology skills and careers.

Inclusiveness

TechHire Miami will be focused on building a coalition of key partners that will work together to put the TechHire foundation in place through this grant vehicle. The Grant is not the ultimate goal, rather the key building block in having our community work more closely to align skill readiness and technology careers in a system that focuses on successful career pathways. By bringing together multiple hiring partners, community organizations, short term training providers, key community organizations, and the Workforce System we can solidify this ecosystem for years to come.

Measureable outcomes

The TechHire Miami community will have an opportunity to leverage best practices already available through the CareerSource system to measure the impact of this effort. The system will allow TechHire to measure and quantify the impact of the investment on the long term increase of income earned by students that enter in technology careers.

Community and employer engagement

Having community and employer engagement will be one of the core principles of the program. Aligning career pathways to in demand careers will be essential. Additionally we will need a system that refreshes the needs of our hiring community on a regular basis so that the career preparation is kept current with the needs of employers. TechHire will work with organizations like the Beacon Council and LaunchCode to maintain a close link to employers and their needs. Additionally TechHire Miami will work closely with community organization to provide disadvantaged individuals with the resources and skills needed to work in 21st century careers.

Focus on technology skills and careers

The focus on in demand skills will be built on providing options for short term high impact career training. This training will be supported by a myriad of providers including the Idea Center at Miami Dade College, The Academy of South Florida, Florida Vocational Institute, IronHack, New Horizons Computer Learning Center of South Florida, and Wyncode. Additionally, additional learning and career readiness tools will be provided from Knack, and ITProTV.
